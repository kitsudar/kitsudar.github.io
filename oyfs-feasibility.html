OYFS Feasibility Study

The Problem

Child welfare caseworkers do critical work, often under difficult circumstances. Leadership for the Office of Youth and Family Services (OYFS) wants to ensure that caseworkers are using the best available data and input from family members to make informed decisions around each case. In order to reach the most positive outcome for children and families, both leadership and caseworkers need more visibility into the progress that each family member is making and the overall state of the case.

The Solution

To boost OYFS's ability to utilize evidence-based practices (EBP) into day-to-day decision making, guiding OYFS caseworkers through daily workflows, applying behavioral science to the OYFS departmentâ€™s policies, provide advanced reporting, rich visual analytics, and comprehensive case planning and tracking to the Office of Youth and Family Services.

The Project

The team worked closely to apply the Design Thinking process to the project. We built empathy by conducting interviews with the caseworkers. I facilitated the mapping activities to empathize, define, and ideate on the solutions. Once we've uncovered the biggest pain points, we defined them into needs statements. We then ideated on those needs statements to come up with viable solutions. We did a user flow diagram to map out the workflow and experience of the app. Low and high fidelity prototypes were created to validate the solutions with our customers.
